Voice-First Leadership

The hardest conversation in your week deserves a rehearsal.

Pupil services runs on conversations that cannot go badly — the declassification meeting, the out-of-district placement denial, the parent who just got the evaluation results. Advanced Voice Mode gives you a place to practice the sentence you don't want to improvise.

01

Rehearse the declassification meeting

Talk through the parent's likely objections before you walk in. Let AI play the parent who is certain their child still needs services. Find the words that honor both the progress and the worry.

02

Debrief after the hard day

Twelve IEP meetings, one crisis call, two hallway conversations that mattered. Drive home and talk it out. Get back the pattern, the one thing to note, the one person to follow up with tomorrow.

03

Practice the evaluation delivery

Role-play the parent meeting where you give the autism classification for the first time. Get the follow-up questions you'll actually face. Find the sentence that lets the family hear you.

The Agentic Frontier

NotebookLM as Specialist on the Pupil Services Team

A notebook with the right sources becomes a domain expert your team can query. Citation-grounded. Never makes things up. Six specialist agents every PPS office should stand up this quarter.

01

The Part 200 Advisor

NYSED Part 200, IDEA regulations, state-issued field memos, and OSEP guidance letters. Tells you what the law actually says about the case on your desk at 4:47 on a Friday.

02

The Case History Librarian

Every IEP, every evaluation, every email with the family for a single student, loaded into one notebook. The chronology your team needs for the annual review — and the due-process preparation if it comes.

03

The McKinney-Vento & Foster Care Desk

Federal law, state guidance, caseworker contact rosters, district transportation protocols. The answer that keeps a family enrolled and a student in the school of origin.

04

The Approved Placement Explorer

SED's approved private placement list, program descriptions, past referrals, and tuition history. When the CSE is considering an out-of-district placement, the due-diligence packet writes itself.

05

The Disproportionality Monitor

Three years of classification and discipline data by race, ethnicity, and classification. Answers whether we have a statistical problem before the state letter says we do.

06

The Grant Program Library

Every awarded and unsuccessful IDEA Part B application, Title III narrative, and mental-health grant. Your next application starts with an honest look at what worked last time.

About this resource

Built for the people who hold the legal contracts for the most vulnerable students.

Directors of Special Education and Assistant Superintendents of Pupil Services run the department where federal law, family grief, union rules, and fiscal reality collide every Tuesday afternoon. Most AI tools built for "school leaders" have nothing specific to offer this job. The generic coaching prompt doesn't help when the parent's letter is on your desk, the 60-school-day clock is running, and the BOCES placement just said no.

This library is organized around the eight domains where pupil services work actually lives — CSE/CPSE chair work, 504 plans, compliance and state reporting, MTSS, related services, McKinney-Vento and equity, staff supervision, and budget and family engagement. Every workflow is scaled by district size, because a director of 450 students is the school psychologist and the BCBA on top of the administrative role, while a director of 40,000 runs a department that looks like a school district of its own.

Copy the prompts. Adapt them to your district. Share them with the director across the lake who just inherited 800 IEPs last month.
